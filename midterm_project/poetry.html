Below is a poem I made about the beauty of Alpenglows:

Some themes that this poem reflects are belonging, struggle, love, hope and empowerment

Shimmery curtains

The house that we built

Upon nothing but bricks and stone

My sweet, scented lilac perfume

The late night talks about our life

Oh, how we've grown

7 in the morning

You ready in your maroon colored dress

The one that I stitched in pearls for you

We are finally having the life that we've always wanted

Then why does it all feel so wrong?

Life would be too confusing if we knew all the answers

Isn’t that what you told me?


The looks and stares that we always get

When after all we are just like everyone else

Human beings who are scared

Scared of what their friends and family will think of them

Scared of what the world will think of them

Scared that the person they once knew is slowly disappearing like each tick on a watch


But in the end we both stay hopeful

That the next alpenglow will bring us both peace

That we can be who we want to be

Without all the judgements in this world

Without the lingering feeling that every second in time together, our lives are getting picked apart

Like someone putting together all the wrong puzzle pieces and realizing that everything they ever made was wrong Out of place


I think it’s truly beautiful

Time and how it heals you, changes you

If only all of us could see that there isn’t room for more hate in this world


But maybe someday in this world we'll finally be able to be acknowledged as humans

And we won't be called mistakes

We won't be shamed

We won't be silenced

We won’t be picked apart

Because it’s okay to fall, it’s okay to break, it’s okay to be hopeless but what it’s never okay to do is to give up


Because no one is truly fearless

Despite how many times they try convincing themselves that they're not afraid of something or that the fear that they once had is gone, it’s always there

And we may never be able to eradicate that aspect of us

Our mistakes that end up leading to the journey

The journey that led us to the alpenglow


Because at the top of that mountain we can finally watch the reddish glow near the sunset on the summit of the mountains illuminate

It will give us the courage to make the jump

We may not know where we will jump but that doesn't matter

Sometimes in life you have to jump, even though you may not know where you're going to land


And finally at that moment we'll be able to hold our hands with pride

We’ll be able to wear clothing where the rainbow hearts of comfort are there to make us feel special, acknowledged

Not as mistakes to this world, but as people who have fears that don't let that stop them from making the most out of their lives



We’ll be able to wave our flags painted in stripes of pink, purple and blue



Whoever thought that one phenomenen could be LIFE CHANGING
